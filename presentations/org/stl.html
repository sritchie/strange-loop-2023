Imagine a world instead where, starting in elementary school, kids encounter geometry, math and physics through interactive "microworlds", or playful simulations designed to explore ideas like motion, balance and gravity. As students matured they'd discover that these microworlds formed the spine of a series of executable textbooks, with the mathematics of ideas like electromagnetism augmented by powerful circuit simulations. Usually-dry mathematics would gain the ability to perform itself for a student, and the student, by taking their notes in this computational environment, would build up a portfolio of tangible, executable code and models over the course of their education.

  (fn [w]
    (- (D (comp ((partial 2) L) (Gamma w)))

Steps

The CHALLENGE is to build something that feels like it has come from a community.

Not your typical codebase.

I've attempted to clear a path toward this vision by:

  • Building Emmy, a state-of-the-art computer algebra system, like Mathematica capable of running in a browser or on a modern workstation
  • Creating a library of modern 2D and 3D visualization plugins capable of exploring ideas in mathematics and physics from elementary school up through MIT-graduate-level of sophistication
  • Building out a notebook interface capable of allowing students to work with powerful mathematical tools in a collaborative way, almost like Google Docs for a computing environment, with instant feedback and access to computing resources
  1. Build a CAS that can bring physics to life
  2. Do it in a way that seeds the culture
  3. Hoist it into the browser
  4. Make it collaborative
  5. Build a community, beyond the glass
